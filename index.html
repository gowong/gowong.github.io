My name is Gordon Wong and since learning to build my first computer when I was 10 years old, I have always been interested in computers and technology. I was drawn to software development after learning computer programming in high school and being surprised at how difficult it was to create the software that we use every day.

I am currently pursuing a combined B.S./M.S. in Computer Science with a concentration in software engineering at University of California, San Diego. My expected graduation date is December 2015. I have gained software engineering experience during my college career by working on web and mobile applications for summer internships and team projects. During my free time, I develop BG Monitor, an app for Android smartphones that helps people with diabetes manage their blood sugar levels. I also enjoy playing video games, playing the guitar, and reading about the latest developments in technology.

Personal Projects

BG Monitor Diabetes Android app - 20,000+ downloads (2012 - Present)

I decided to create BG Monitor back in 2011 after noticing a lack of easy to use diabetes apps on Android. My unique perspective on diabetes allowed me to develop a simple yet powerful app that helps diabetics manage their blood sugar levels. BG Monitor encourages users to record their blood sugar readings, amount of food consumed, and exercise activity. The app uses this information to help users identify trends so that they know how to keep their blood sugar levels in range.

Employment

Intuit Software Engineering Internship – Mountain View, CA (Summer 2013)

The Intuit Online Payroll app lets small business owners pay their employees, complete tax forms, and view pay history from their mobile devices. During my internship, I collaborated with software engineers and designers to develop new features, fix existing bugs, and implement a visual redesign of the Android Payroll app. My work was included in the version 4.4 update that was released to thousands of users on October 16, 2013.

Cisco Team Internship – San Jose, CA (Summer 2012)

Many of Cisco's networking products are incredibly energy efficient, but it turns out that customers rarely take energy efficiency into account when making a purchase. I collaborated with other engineering interns to create an interactive exhibit that convinces customers to purchase Cisco's energy efficient products. The exhibit shows the amount of money that could be saved by owning devices with lower power consumption rates. My work was focused on designing and implementing the exhibit's touchscreen interface and developing an application to collect and analyze the power consumption rates of the networking devices.

Course Projects

Web Analytics Application (Team project, Spring 2013)

The goal of this project was to create a shopping website that provided analytics on millions of product sales. Since there was so much information that needed to be aggregated and analyzed, we focused heavily on database optimization in order to produce analytics quickly and efficiently. We also implemented a product management feature for shop owners and product ordering for shoppers.

I Lost It website (Team project, Fall 2012)

I Lost It helps users find their lost items. Users post a description of their item, where they think they lost it, and their contact information. Other users who spotted or found the item can contact the owner to return the item. I collaborated with five other students to develop the website and focused on implementing the website's search and item categorization features.

Food Nutrition Android app (Team project, Winter 2012)

This was the first collaborative software project that I worked on and my team consisted of 10 students. We developed an Android app that lets users track their food consumption and record details about food nutrition. The project was an important learning experience as I practiced working with others and following software development procedure which included documenting system requirements, use cases, and test plans.
